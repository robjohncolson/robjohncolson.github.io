Thai Green Papaya Salad (Som Tum)

Ingredients

  • 1 ½ cups julienned green papaya, soaked in ice water for 10-15 minutes
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • Thai chilies, to taste
  • 1 ½ Tbsp palm sugar, finely chopped, packed
  • 2-3 long beans, cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 3 Tbsp roasted peanuts
  • 1 heaping tablespoon small dried shrimp, roughly chopped if large
  • 2 Tbsp fresh lime juice
  • 2 tsp tamarind paste
  • 1 ½ Tbsp fish sauce
  • ½ cup grape tomato halves or small tomato wedges
  • Serving suggestion: sticky rice and BBQ chicken

Instructions

  1. Drain the soaked papaya well and place in a bowl lined with paper towels to absorb excess water.
  2. In a mortar and pestle, pound garlic and chilies until smooth.
  3. Add palm sugar and mash until dissolved into a wet paste.
  4. Add long beans and pound until broken.
  5. Add dried shrimp and peanuts, pounding to slightly break the peanuts.
  6. Add fish sauce, tamarind paste, and lime juice. Stir to mix and dissolve the sugar.
  7. Add papaya and tomatoes, mixing using the pound-and-flip method until well combined and tomatoes slightly crushed.
  8. Plate and sprinkle with remaining peanuts. Serve with sticky rice and BBQ chicken for a classic meal.

Notes

  • Roast your own peanuts in a 350°F (175°C) oven for 15-18 minutes, stirring halfway through for even roasting.
  • If you have a small mortar and pestle, transfer the dressing to a mixing bowl before adding papaya and tomatoes.
  • If you don’t have a mortar and pestle, use alternative methods like mincing and mashing ingredients in a mixing bowl.
